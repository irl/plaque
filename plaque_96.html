Commemorative Plaques Record Details

Grant and Geddes

Location : Chapel Court, off Castle Street (Castlegate)

Area : Central Aberdeen

Plaque Type :Non-standard / Memorial

About Grant and Geddes :Catholic Bishops. Grant was the fourth Catholic priest in Aberdeen following the Reformation. By the 1770s his growing congregation needed a permanent place of worship. Land around this site was bought in 1771 upon which a chapel and house was erected. Geddes, one time rector of Scalan College, was instrumental, along with Priest Gordon, in arranging the erection of St Peter’s Chapel. Geddes was uncle to Priest Gordon.

More Information : This plaque is on the site of the house wherein died Bishop James Grant, on 3 December 1778 and Bishop John Geddes, 11 February 1799. Bishop James Grant was the fourth Catholic priest in Aberdeen following the Reformation in 1560: the first was Robert Strachan in 1686. Bishop James Grant was vicar apostolic of the Lowland district of Scotland. This title is given to a bishop in a country where the normal hierarchy is not established and whose powers are delegated through the Sacred Congregation of Propaganda. Scotland remained under vicars apostolic until 1878. In the later 1760s Bishop Grant lodged with a hatmaker, Patrick Leslie, in the Gallowgate. At this time Mass was said in the priest’s lodging or in a room hired for that purpose. As the congregation grew there was an increasing need for a larger meeting place. The reason given for this increase in the congregation was, according to Bishop Grant, an influx of people from the hinterland and woolcombers from Ireland. In 1771-2 land and a house were bought in the Castlegate, near the Justice Port: this was the ‘Lodging closs and garden of the heirs of Patrick Smith of Inveramsay…’. Both buildings on the site were demolished and new chapel and house erected which Bishop Grant moved into in 1774. The new building was composed of a chapel on the ground floor and accommodation on the first floor. It was then the chief residence of the Vicars Apostolic, and according to Gammie in The Churches of Aberdeen a ‘chief centre of the faith in the north.’ Bishop John Geddes was born in the Mains of Corridoun on 9 September 1735. His sister was mother to Charles Gordon (Priest Gordon) who was the first Priest in Charge (1803-1854) of St Peter’s Roman Catholic Church in Aberdeen. At the age of 23 he was ordained on 18 March 1759. In 1762 he was appointed rector of Scalan College, which he reorganised and at which he had building works undertaken. He wrote an historical account of the college there which can be found in Innes Review (Vol. XIV No. 2 1963). In 1770 he was sent to Spain to deal with the ongoing issue of the expulsion of the Jesuits and to attempt to re-establish a Scots College: he remained in Spain until 1780. Immediately prior to leaving Spain in 1780 on 30 November Geddes was consecrated as Bishop of Morocco and coadjutor, or auxiliary, to Bishop George Hay. It was through the efforts of Bishop Hay and Priest Gordon that St Peter’s Church was erected in what became known as Chapel Court in 1803. Prior to this it was known as Inveramsay’s Close. Bishop Geddes resigned in 1797 and died two years later on 11 February 1799. He is buried in the same plot at the Snow Church, Old Aberdeen, as Bishop Grant and his nephew Priest Gordon. It is unclear if any of the 1772-4 building in what was Inveramsay’s Close survives today. If it was knocked down at the time when St Peter’s Church was erected then in fact the plaque marks the site of the house rather than the house itself. Unfortunately it is not known when or by whom this plaque was erected.
