Commemorative Plaques Record Details

Blockhouse (1497 - 1832)

Location : Pocra Quay

Area : North Aberdeen

Plaque Type :Non-standard / Memorial

About Blockhouse :This commemorates the blockhouse which was Aberdeen’s first harbour defence battery. A round lime and mortar building with several cannons, it was located strategically at the entry to the harbour and served as the first line of defence from a naval attack for several centuries. When a new battery was built in 1781 the blockhouse was leased out to various companies including the Greenland Whaling Company. The plaque states that a wooden fort stood on that spot in 1477, this has not been proven. The plaque finally states ‘1832 This Building Erected’, this refers to a fish workers’ tenement, which was later demolished. This was erected by Dr Alexander Walker.
